differential equations and their vector fields



every point in the plane has a location.

let's call this location (x,y).


the differential equation tells us where this point wants to move to:
    if dy = 0 and
  1. dx = 1, the point wants to move towards the right at a speed of 1
  2. dx = -2, the point wants to move towards the left at a speed of 2
    3. if dx = 0 and
  3. dy = 1, the point wants to move upwards at a speed of 1
  4. dy = -3, the point wants to move downwards at a speed of 3
